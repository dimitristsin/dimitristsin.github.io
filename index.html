About

Hello! I'm Dimitrios Tsintsilidas, a passionate first-year PhD student in Computer Science at the University of Warwick.

I'm a proud member of the Theory and Foundations (FoCS) group and the Centre for Discrete Mathematics and its Applications (DIMAP), where I'm advised by Igor Carboni Oliveira and Christian Ikenmeyer. My research journey is generously supported by the Chancellors' International Scholarship.

My academic background spans both pure mathematics and engineering. I completed my studies in Pure Mathematics (Algebra & Logic) at Aristotle University of Thessaloniki. Prior to that, I earned my Diploma in Electrical & Computer Engineering, culminating in a thesis on Quantum Cryptography conducted at LIP6, CNRS/Sorbonne University.

What I'm currently exploring:

  • Connections between Proof Complexity and Bounded Arithmetic
  • Applications of algebraic methods in circuit lower bounds
  • Quantum information theory and its implications for complexity
